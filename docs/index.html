In detail

During my highschool years I started to work as a web developer for a local web agency on a casual basis.

After working for a year as a part-time IT Administrator at medel.com, a friend and myself decided to star our own business, which offered webdesign and development. We were able to finish several medium sized projects.

In 2007 I got my first full-time jobs as a web developer at styleflasher.at.

Working for clients like Red Bull and Eglo, allowed me to get more experience working on bigger projects and strengthen my skills around writing quality code.

In 2008 I moved to Australia and continued working previous company (remotely) and started working with thesquad.com.au on a freelance basis.

In 2011 I got the opportunity to work as a full-time front-end developer for worldnomads.com.

Exposure to larger scale and high traffic websites like tid.com.au, worldnomads.com.au and suresave.com.au, helped me get a better understanding of applying various optimization techniques to improve page performance.

Being part of a big team and within an agile environment introduced me to the challenges around managing time, estimates and expectations.

In 2013 I was offered a contracting role at NewsCorp Australia.

When I first started I worked within the team responsible for integrating various social networking features and enhancing our users personal experience (eg.: article bookmarking, reading history).

With millions of page views per day and hence page performance being a priority, strenghened my JavaScript skills and deepen my knowledge around performance related areas.

After half a year I got offered a full-time role and was promoted to a lead front-end position. I was also assigned to the team, who looks after the masthead sites (Daily Telegraph, Herald Sun ...).

Some of my responsibilities where: mentoring, architectural and code-quality guidance to junior developers, automation, stackeholder & cross-team communction and deployment management.

After a little more than 2 years at NewsCorp I was approached by the CEO of Chuffed.org was offered a role as a senior front end developer.
Being touched by his vision to create a platform that helps people raise money for socially benefitial projects I was excited to join.

When I started I was faced with the challenge of having to redo and rearchitect a lot of the codebase, introduce standards and best practices - like automated browser testing.

Due to a team restructure I am currently leading the product team, consisting of a UX/Designer and two developers.

In my spare time I build crazy lego contructs with my son, climb on rocks and feed my cat (he makes me do that a lot).
